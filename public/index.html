⚠ Analyst note — forward book unwind: a one-time effect
The RBI's $100M net open position cap forced banks to unwind an estimated $30–40B in long-dollar positions, temporarily strengthening the rupee and slowing reserve depletion. This is a one-time effect.

Going forward, every market participant — corporates, importers, fund managers, and households — will rationally seek to build their own dollar buffers given the rupee's trajectory. This structural shift in dollar demand means the RBI cannot rely on regulatory squeezes to defend the currency repeatedly. Each successive intervention tool loses potency once the market adapts.

With the forward book at a record $103B, the RBI faces a compounding problem: future contract maturities require dollar purchases that will themselves pressure the rupee, creating a feedback loop that only a fundamental improvement in the current account or a sustained capital inflow reversal can break.
