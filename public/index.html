Analyst note - forward book unwind: a one-time effect
The RBI cap forced banks to unwind an estimated $30-40B in long-dollar positions, temporarily strengthening the rupee. This is a one-time effect.

Going forward, every market participant will rationally build their own dollar buffers, creating structural dollar demand. Each successive RBI intervention tool loses potency once the market adapts.

With the forward book at a record $103B, future contract maturities require dollar purchases that will themselves pressure the rupee, creating a feedback loop only a current account improvement or capital inflow reversal can break.
